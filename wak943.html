The boat either becomes seaworthy or it doesn't. The distance either closes or it doesn't.

The boat leaks. The deck is rotting underfoot. The mast stands on wood already going soft. A father is on one side of the world. A daughter is on the other. Fix the boat. Go. Come back. Same harbour, both times.

Not just to arrive. To come back a different man than the one who left.

Not the only way there. The one with heart in it.

It's a vessel, not a possession. A faster boat, same crossing, works just as well. So does the man sailing it — a passenger, same as the boat.

The crossing has to be made alone. That was never the same thing as unaccompanied. Anyone who already knows what it is to be a passenger in their own life knows the difference too — a company can mean it just as much as a person can.
