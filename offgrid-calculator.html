Solar sizing

array W = (daily Wh ÷ sun hours) ÷ (1 − derate). Sun hours are the real lever — the same loads need roughly 5× more panel in a Norwegian winter than a Mediterranean summer.

Battery sizing

bank Wh = daily Wh × autonomy days ÷ usable DoD, then divided by system voltage for amp-hours. Lithium's higher usable DoD means a noticeably smaller, lighter bank than lead-acid for the same runtime.

Losses compound

Wiring, charge controller, and inverter losses all stack on top of each other — a system with 20% total loss needs a fifth more solar and battery than the raw load numbers suggest.
